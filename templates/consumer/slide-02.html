The Problem

We take 1,000 photos. We remember 10.

Last year, I scrolled through my camera roll looking for photos from my daughter's first birthday.

I had 847 photos from that month.

I couldn't find them. They were buried under screenshots, memes, and random shots.

The moments that matter most are drowning in digital noise.

And when we finally find them, we've forgotten the context—who was there, what was happening, why it mattered.
